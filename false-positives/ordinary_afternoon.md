# A Perfectly Ordinary Afternoon (contains no cryptography)

In a quiet garden of camellia blooms and serpentine hedges, Simon brewed a
pot of tea and had a brilliant idea. A falcon circled overhead while a
rabbit nibbled a grain of wheat beside the whirlpool fountain. He admired
the aesthetic of a Caesar salad, hummed a salsa tune, and watched a ghost
of mist rise over the Andes.

His designated hitter days behind him, Simon now studies malaria maps in
Bavaria, files old films of psalms under "realms", and rides his bike past
the marsh to a teddy bears' picnic. Frodo the beagle chased a rainbow while
the local ECB (the European Central Bank) aired a forecast on CBC.

None of this is cryptography. Every capitalized word that resembles a
cipher -- Simon, Falcon, Serpent, Tiger, Rainbow, Picnic, Saber, Kyber,
Dilithium -- is just a name, a plant, a pet, or a movie reference.
